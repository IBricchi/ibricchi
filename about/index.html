    oesia

    oesia.com
    oesia home page

    OESIA is a multi-national coorperation based in Madrid Spain. During the summer of 2018, I was employed there for an internship. My duties were to automate a system for identifieng employees who's work could potentially apply for goverment sanctioned tax-breaks for the company. The sytem would also allow for easy collection of data to to proove that the employee did infact apply for tax-breaks, in the event of an audit.

    oesia logo

    The company had a heavy emphasis on inovation. The government of spain recenlty had created a new law, decreasing the ammount of social-secuirty a company had to pay to the government for workers who worked in a project which fell under certain guidelens to do with techonolical inovation. As such the company, could largely benefit from taking advantage of this law.

    Using a combination of data from human resources, employee work hours, project lists, etc. I devised a system which would identify known emplyees who had already been marked as able to take advantage of the new law, and through extrapolation found projects, which could be considered "invoative", and generated a list of potential candidates.

    This system massively decreased the list of potential candidates down to a managable list. And sorted them from most-likely to apply to least likely. A human could go through, see the evidence collected, recieve identification information to look for more proof, and then either accept, or decline them.

    Once in the system, automated emails would be sent to those who were deemed able to apply for the tax cuts, to submit evidence of work. A small amount of checking was automated to ensure that the information was valid, however, all information had to be vetted by someone, just to ensure that there was valid proof of work.

    Unfortunately I am unable to provide any of the code for this project, as I was not allowed to keep a copy of it, nor would I have been allowed to publish it.

    Borro, is a London based financial statrtup working with high value asset based lending. Durring the summer of 2017 I was epmployed to run an experiment using blockchain as a potential new financial ledger for the company. The technology would allow for permanent financial records, it would increase trust in the records, and for a company dealing with large assets such as Borro, increasing trust is no small matter.

    Borro logo

    Using multichain (a software which allows creation and usage of a block chain), I created a small local blockchain server. Around it I created a webserver using Java, PHP, and JS, in the end I created a simple web page which allowed a simple user interface to read, and write into the financial ledger. It had querying abilities, for reading, and would check information written into it before committing anything into the ledger, ensuring all information was correct, as far as it could tell.

    Towards the end of the project a small report was written on the findings, and in the end the company decided not to go with the block chain proposal, the current system was good enough, and it was deemed an unnessary addition.
